Week 3

Monday: Today I participated in the repair of a worn-out roller shaft used in an industrial machine. I inspected the damaged component to determine the extent of wear and material loss caused by continuous operation under load. I cleaned the surface thoroughly using a wire brush and grinder to remove rust, oil, and old weld deposits to ensure proper bonding during welding. I then built up the worn section using arc welding by depositing layers of weld metal while maintaining correct electrode angle, arc length, and travel speed. After welding, I allowed the component to cool naturally before grinding it to restore a smooth finish and inspecting it for defects such as cracks and porosity.
Tuesday: Today I practiced TIG welding on mild steel components under supervision. I prepared the workpieces by cleaning the surfaces to remove impurities that could affect weld quality. I set up the TIG machine, selected the appropriate tungsten electrode, and adjusted gas flow. During welding, I focused on maintaining a stable arc and steady hand movement while controlling heat input. I learned how to produce clean, precise welds with minimal spatter and distortion.
Wednesday: Today I fabricated a rectangular steel frame using square hollow sections. I studied the drawing to understand dimensions and material requirements. I measured, marked, and cut the steel accurately before removing burrs. I assembled the pieces on a flat surface, checked squareness, and tack welded joints before completing final welding. The finished frame was inspected for accuracy and alignment.
Thursday: Today I carried out grinding and finishing of fabricated components. I removed excess weld metal, sharp edges, and surface defects using an angle grinder. I prepared surfaces for painting by ensuring they were clean and smooth. I also inspected the final product for dimensional accuracy and weld quality.
Friday: Today I performed maintenance of welding equipment, including cleaning arc and MIG welding machines, inspecting cables, electrode holders, and gas hoses. I also organized tools and ensured proper workshop housekeeping and safe disposal of scrap materials.
